## 1. The ask

> At the end of this meeting, the audience will: **[decide / approve / commit to / understand] _______________**.

If you cannot complete this sentence, stop. Without it, no storyline is possible.

## 2. The single sentence

> The deck argues: **_______________________**.

This is the top of the pyramid. It will appear on slide 1, in the title.

## 3. The hardest objection

> The objection most likely to derail the recommendation: **_______________________**.
> The on-slide answer: **_______________________**.

If the answer is not yet defensible, the recommendation needs more work before the deck is built.

## 5. The three reasons

The recommendation rests on three (or four) MECE reasons. Each must, on its own, support the recommendation.

1. **Reason 1:** _______________________
2. **Reason 2:** _______________________
3. **Reason 3:** _______________________

If you have more than four, you have not yet found the real grouping. If you have one, commit to a one-pillar argument and say so.

## 6. Action-title outline (horizontal logic)

Draft every slide title before composing any slide body. Read top to bottom — the titles alone should tell the story.

| # | Tag | Title (a complete sentence stating the conclusion) | Archetype |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | DECISION | [Recommendation, in one sentence] | BLUF |
| 2 | DISCUSSION | [Set up via SCQA — situation + complication] | Context |
| 3 | DISCUSSION | [Reason 1, stated as a conclusion] | Evidence |
| 4 | DISCUSSION | [Evidence supporting reason 1] | Evidence |
| 5 | DISCUSSION | [Reason 2, stated as a conclusion] | Evidence |
| 6 | DISCUSSION | [Evidence supporting reason 2] | Evidence |
| 7 | DISCUSSION | [Reason 3, stated as a conclusion] | Evidence |

| 10 | DECISION | [The ask, restated, with owner and date] | Asks & Decisions |

(Default ceiling: 10 main-body slides. Justify any expansion explicitly.)

## 7. Horizontal-logic check

Read titles 1–10 aloud. Answer in writing:

- Does it form a coherent argument? (yes / no — and which slides break the chain)
- Is the recommendation present in slides 1–3? (yes / no)
- Could a stranger reading only titles summarise the ask in one sentence? (yes / no)

If any answer is no, fix titles before composing slide bodies.

## 9. Self-review against the three tests

Before composing slides, walk every row of section 6 through:

- [ ] Title is a conclusion, not a label.
- [ ] The slide can land its sentence with one chart, one table, or one short text block.
- [ ] For every recommendation-bearing slide, the hardest objection has an on-slide treatment.

Any "no" → fix the storyline before building slides.
